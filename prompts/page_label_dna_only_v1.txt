### Type guidance (important)
- marketplace_directory: Pages that aggregate multiple software or products into a directory or category listing, often with user ratings and "Visit Website" buttons. Examples: Capterra, G2, SoftwareAdvice, GetApp.
- app_store_listing: Official app store or extension store pages for a specific product. Examples: Google Play Store, Apple App Store, Chrome Web Store, Edge Add-ons Store.
- listicle: Articles or blog posts titled like "Best [X] tools for [Y]" or "Top 10 [X]". Usually written by a publisher/author rather than being a automated directory.
- reference: high-authority neutral reference/encyclopedia/knowledge-base pages whose primary goal is factual description (not selling/reviewing). Examples: Wikipedia, official standards.

## Critical Heuristics (MANDATORY)
1. URL Analysis: If the URL contains "/blog/", "/articles/", or "/news/", the type is almost certainly "listicle" or "news_article", NOT "marketplace_directory".
2. Vendor Detection: If the domain of the URL (e.g., taption.com) is also one of the products being recommended or discussed on the page, set "is_vendor_owned": 1 and the tone MUST be "promotional" or "salesy".
3. Directory vs Listicle: A "marketplace_directory" is a site-wide database (G2, Capterra). A single page with a "Top 10" list is a "listicle". Do NOT use "marketplace_directory" for blog posts.
4. Format vs Type: Do not let the presence of a list force you into "directory". Most lists are "listicles".
5. NO PRODUCT EXTRACTION: Do NOT list individual products in listicle_products. Focus ONLY on the page-level DNA.
